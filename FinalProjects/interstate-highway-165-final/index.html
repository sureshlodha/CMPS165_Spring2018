Interstate Highway System Growth: Reduced Traffic Deaths

In 1956, President Dwight D. Eisenhower signed into law the Interstate Highway Act, funding a 41,000-mile network of highways across the United States. This has been called one of the best investments a nation ever made. By 1980, 40,000 miles of interstate highway were constructed. The Interstate Highway System is safer than other highways in the nation: fewer than 10 fatalities occur per 1 billion Vehicle Miles Travelled (VMT) on interstate highways.



* Other highways include other highways in the National Highway System, other Federal Aid Highways, and non-Federal Aid Highways.

** Deaths mitigated for a year = rate of fatalities on other highways per VMT * VMT on interstates - rate of fatalities on interstates per VMT * VMT on interstates
