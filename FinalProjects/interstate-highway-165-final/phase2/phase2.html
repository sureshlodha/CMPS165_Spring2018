The Interstate Highway System: the Road to a Growing Nation

Gabriel Hartman ghartman@ucsc.edu

Purpose

In 1956, President Dwight D. Eisenhower signed into law the Interstate Highway Act, fundind a 41,000-mile network of highways across the United States. This has been called one of the best investments a nation ever made. The amount of money spent on the interstate highway system was returned in safety, economic gains, and improved quality of life for Americans. I want to show that federally funded investments to public infrastructure such as the Interstate Highway System can be cost effective and yield useful returns for the nation.

Data Design

Data Variable 1: growth of the interstate highway system over time. I want to show how long and in which parts of the nation the Interstate Highway System was throughout its construction from 1956-1996 (perhaps some years before and after for context.) The data for this will have to come from multiple sources: one for the span of the highway itself (modern data will suffice) and another data source stating when different parts of the interstate highway were constructed. The first dataset was compiled by somebody and can be found on github. The fine folks at Wikipedia have compiled some of the second dataset, although I'd love to see if I can find a more detailed dataset showing the parts within each highway being constructed over a few years as the first visualization source below shows.

Data Variable 2: Vehicle Accident and Death data. the Federal Highway Administration has this data.

Data Variable 3: Commute time and method data. The U.S. Census has this data

Data Variable 4: The amount spent and the sources of the revenue going towards the interstate highway system. Unfortunately it doesn't look like anybody has put the yearly information all in one place, but it's all available in the yearly budgets for the U.S. Government (example: 1957)

Data Variable 5: Population Density by State over time - from the U.S. Census

Visualization Design

The main visualization layout will be a geomap, with a timeline slider to show various dates (perhaps show by decade, due to the nature of the census data). Data variables can be shown on the map mapped to colors by category.

To show expenditures on the highway system, the timeline can show on it a line chart of highway expenditures by year (adjusted for inflation). (Like in this visualization example.)

There will be a panel to select what data variable to overlay on top of the highway growth change over time between the non-highway data variables: commute data, vehicle accident data, and population data.

To show expenditures, each individual highway will be hoverable, bolding the highway selected. Hovering a particular highway will show a tooltip on the side indicating what highway it is, the cost of constructing that highway, the years during which it was under initial construction, and the areas it services.
