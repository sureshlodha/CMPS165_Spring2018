Education and Voting Power of Hispanics

This data visualization is based off an article written for Pew Research Center by Jen Manuel Krogstad about hispanics and education(Link).

The data for this visulization was taken from Pew Research Center by Antonio Flores, Gustavo Lopez, and Jynnah Radford.(Link).

This visualization shows how important education is to the hispanic community and how over recent years it has improved. For example, the high school dropout rate has significantly dropped, college enrollment is on the up, and much more. Not only are education rates displayed but also voting trends. The data for this was also taken from Pew Research Center(Link). As the population rises, the hispanic communities' voting power has also increased. With more hispanics getting to the polls means more representation and more influence over public policy and elected officials.
