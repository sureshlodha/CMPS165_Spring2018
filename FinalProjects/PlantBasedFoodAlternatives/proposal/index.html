Those looking to switch to a vegetarian, pescatarian, or vegan diet often have concerns whether or not they can stay on a healthy diet, without indulging in more expensive premium food products. Our visualization will provide the ease-of-access that doesn't come naturally with researching nutritional facts and substitutionsâ€”by doing so in a visual manner.

The purpose of this visualization is to give insight into how people can easily substitute animal products in their diet to plant-based alternatives. We intend to show how a diet with limited or no animal products can still be nutritious, flavorful, and affordable.

Data Sources & Variables

The data variables we plan to use in the visualization include: iron, protein, omega-3 and omega-6 fats, vitamin B12, vitamin D, and calcium. These are generally the greatest nutritional concerns for people who are considering switching to a plant-based diet. To achieve our purpose of showing the data to give food alternatives we are setting up a visualization design that will allow us to compare food with similar nutritional breakdowns side by side. One side will have food from a non limited balanced diet while the other side will have vegan or vegetarian alternatives (this will be toggled with a filter).

Our display will also have a filter in order to change the nutrient that we are interested in displaying at the time. These nutrients will be from our data of nutrients that vegans and vegetarians frequently struggle getting enough of. Each item displayed on our visualization will have a tooltip with additional nutritional information along with serving size and calories
