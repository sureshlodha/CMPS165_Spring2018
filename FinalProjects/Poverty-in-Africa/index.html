Poverty and HDI in Africa

This visualization displays the progress these African countries have made in the last 20 years. We are able to see a decrease in poverty rate along with a steady imporovment in the standard of living, knowledge and life expectancy throughout the continent all while population continues to grow.

Human Development Index (HDI): a composite statistic of life expectancy, education, and per capita income indicators, which are used to rank countries into four tiers of human development.

Gini Coefficient: a statistical measure of the degree of variation or inequality represented in a set of values. A higher value means more inequality.
